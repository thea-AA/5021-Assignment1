40% Consider the discrete-time asset allocation example in section 8.4 of Rao and Jelvis.

Consider the price vector X, which has dimension n, n> 2.  X(0) = 1. The one period return of asset k is N( a(k), s(k) ), where a(k) is the mean, s(k) is the variance. You are given an initial portfolio with asset k having a portion of p(k), where p(0) is the portion on cash. Obviously, p(0)+ p(1)+…+p(n) = 1. The interest on any cash is r. You can at most adjust 10% of your portfolio at each period. You have an absolute risk averse utility function. Use RL to compute the optimal strategy at each period for any reasonable r, a(k), s(k) and p(k). Show that your program can work for any time horizon < 10, and n< 5.
